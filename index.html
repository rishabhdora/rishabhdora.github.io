About

I am a PhD student in the Department of Chemistry at the University of Rochester, working in the group of Prof. Ignacio Franco with main focus on light-matter interactions and quantum transport.

Before Rochester, I completed a BS-MS (Dual Degree) in Physics from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Bhopal, where I investigated polaron transport in optomechanical arrays as part of my MS thesis under supervison of Prof. Sebastian Wuester .

My work spans the development of numerical simulation software — from finite-difference time-domain (FDTD) electromagnetic simulations for spatially varying laser fields, to time-dependent quantum transport using nonequilibrium Green's function (NEGF) methods — deployed on high-performance computing clusters using parallelized Fortran/Python codes.
